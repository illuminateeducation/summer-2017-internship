Learn About the Illuminate World Changers

Our Story

Illuminate World Changers has introduced a variety of opportunities for Illuminators to give back to their local communities and to the world. IWC was first founded in 2014. Since then, Illuminators have devoted their time to helping students who are in need by becoming involved in projects and organizations like LCC. Although IWC has not received any awards, its members are recognized for their leadership and ideas of how to give back.

Main Goal

The main objective of Illuminate World Changers (IWC) is to make positive impacts and changes in the world. People involved in IWC recognize they are truly blessed and privileged. These principles are applied to their work helping communities in less industrialized countries. Individuals, such as Jim Yang who has been part of the IWC for approximately a year and a half, has helped families in poverty. Love and care for the world are demonstrated by the team of IWC with the construction of a house and gift drives for families. To help the world that we inhabit, "think about what you love, your strengths and advantages and how you can apply them to the world." ~Jim Yang

Projects

Illuminators unite to donate resources and their time to help less privileged individuals. It is not simply about giving away money, it's about devoting one's self and time to participate in projects that will impact people lives and communities locally and globally.



Building Houses

IWC members help the community in Tijuana by building homes for families that are in need. They also provided a church with a new taxi to fund breakfast and lunch programs for students at the school.

An Education

An education is not a privilege in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. IWC has partnered with Africa New Day in the city of Goma to raise funds to help provide children with an education and a bright future.

Gift Drives

Illuminate is all about bonding as a family and making holidays a little brighter for families in need. Every employee at Illuminate becomes a part of the experience by putting time and resources towards gifts for families in need.


Tutoring

The Lighthouse Community Center (LCC) in Santa Ana, CA offers after school tutoring to children. The mission of LCC is to catalyze engagement by equipping community leaders and volunteers to help break the cycles of poverty.

Supporting The Arts

As Illuminators were incorporating artwork into its office space at Illuminate Education, its members decided to have an entire display of artwork created by some of the students Illuminate Education reached with its product. IWC donated art supplies to Magnolia High School.

Burma

IWC is supporting staff in Burma through direct financial contributions and will be working to help build capacity around the organization's educational model, such as stronger professional development for teachers.
